Apartment Hunters: Manhattan Edition

Where can the average New Yorker afford to live?

By Megan Wordell // 2-19-23

They say that if you can make it here you can make it anywhere — but really, can anyone actually make it here? The first time I told someone my rent in the Upper West Side neighborhood of Manhattan I heard the classic:

“You pay THAT much for a bedroom? Well, with that kind of money you could buy a whole farm in Meridian, Mississippi with that kind of cash!!”

But fortunately (unfortunately?), I don’t live in the rural South. I live sandwiched between two of the most beautiful parks — Riverside and Central — and on the edge of multiple MTA stops that can take me pretty much anywhere I want to go for $2.75. I love living in NYC and my partner, Lawrence, and I are looking to put down roots here and really invest in our community.

I moved to Manhattan almost a year ago with Lawrence, our two friends/roommates, and two cats, and our lease is up this Spring. While we love our friends, he and I are excited to move to a one bedroom apartment and really craft a space of our own. But the question is: can we afford to do so?

NYC apartment hunting is notoriously a stressful experience, but figuring out how much one can afford budget-wise can be just as disheartening. The Federal Reserve says that renters should aim to spend no more than 30% of their income on housing, and those who spend upwards of that metric fall under the category “rent burdened.”

With this in mind, I had a central question:

Are there any areas in Manhattan where one person can rent a one bedroom apartment and not be “rent burdened?”
