Sh*t Out of Luck: Bathroom Accessibility in New York City


You're trudging through McCarren park, 2 Variety cold brew coffees in when the feeling hits you. You need to find a bathroom and time is running out. We've all have been there... or I hope it's not just me...

While public bathrooms may seem like a silly story to start my semester off with, I find it’s a pretty nuanced topic. While bathroom habits and practices in the city may seem like a great equalizer, ones ability to walk into an establishment and use the restroom free of charge, anxiety, and hinderance is a mark of privilege. It’s a topic I’m interested in — especially as NYC will soon begin reopening public restrooms at certain MTA stops. It's also something I definitely miss from my hometown, Jacksonville, Florida. While I was Christmas shopping over break and needed a restroom, it was super easy to find one, if not several, at my local outdoor mall. I also remember distictly noticing a difference in accessibility of restrooms in Jacksonville. They were significantly more spactious...and honestly much cleaner.
