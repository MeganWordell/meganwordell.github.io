Nail technicians push for more workplace safety measures after ventilation regulations implemented

At a small salon off Jamaica Avenue in Queens, Maria Victoriano is both a nail technician and accidental chemist. While painting nails and assembling acrylic sets for 10 hours a day for the last 16 years, she interacts with hazardous chemicals the nail industry calls the “toxic trio:” toluene, formaldehyde, and dibutyl phthalate. Numerous studies have linked these chemicals to asthma, allergies, and pregnancy loss — all of which Victoriano says she has experienced.

Pre-existing Health Disparities Could Affect COVID-19's Impact In Rural Communities

If it weren’t for the “hospital heroes” signs strewn on the side of State Road 100, you might not even notice the global pandemic. Social distancing isn’t anything new in Union County. With churches appearing to outnumber houses in certain areas, maintaining a 6-foot distance from your neighbors is just part of another day that ends in “y.” But while the layout and lifestyle of rural north central Florida may seem to quell the spread of COVID-19 at first glance, underlying pre-existing health disparities have caused researchers to question the pandemic’s true impacts on rural communities.
